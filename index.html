The Mission

Foundation models made advanced AI usable by anyone — you simply talk to it, and it works. Robotics isn't there yet. Robots are multiplying and will soon share our streets, homes, and workplaces, yet operating them still demands deep, specialized expertise. My goal is to close that gap — to make commanding a team of robots as natural as prompting a language model, so the people who actually need them aren't locked out by complexity.

I believe the most valuable thing robots can give people is independence: the ability to secure your home, tend your land, and look after what's yours — without depending on labor that's scarce, expensive, or simply unavailable. "I'll protect what's mine" shouldn't require an army. It should require the robots you already own, working for you.

And my bet is on cooperation. A single robot is narrow and fragile; a team of robots — coordinating, sharing context, covering each other's blind spots — can accomplish what no individual machine can. The hard problem was never one perfect robot. It's getting many imperfect ones to act as one.

But a fleet is only as capable as the robots in it. So I care just as much about the individual machines — especially drones and mobile robots — and about how heterogeneous robots, each with different bodies and abilities, can master their own task before they ever cooperate. Getting one robot to do its job well is the foundation everything else is built on.

This matters most where people are hardest to hire: security and guarding, agriculture, large residential and facility management, and factory floors — and in the extreme, search-and-rescue and defense, where sending a team of machines instead of people can be the difference that saves lives.

Make operating a fleet of robots as easy as talking to an LLM — and use it to give people back their independence.

Hardware

A drone built from scratch — frame to flight controller. You can't orchestrate robots you don't understand from the hardware up.

Turning a drone's dead-weight landing gear into a gripper, so it can grasp and carry objects mid-flight.
In Progress
